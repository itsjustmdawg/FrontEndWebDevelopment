Courses Offered @ SP

These courses are all part of the School Of Computing of Singapore Polytechnic as of

Diploma in Applied AI and Analytics (S30) Common ICT Programme (S32) Diploma in Infocomm Security Management (S54) Diploma in Information Technology (S69)
The Diploma in Applied Artificial Intelligence and Analytics (DAAA) is a 3-year full-time diploma course offered by the School of Computing in Singapore Polytechnic. Through this course, students will be able to develop the necessary skills to thrive in the Artificial Intelligence domain. The modules that students will have to take include Fundamentals of Programming, Statistics for Data Science, Programming for Data Analytics, Data Visualisation etc. Students in Year 2 or Year 3 of the course, will be able to opt for the Industry Now Curriculum (INC) Pathway. After graduating with a Diploma in AI and Analytics, graduates will be to look forward to exciting career options such as AI Applications Developer, Data Analyst, Data Scientist and many more. The Common ICT Programme (DCITP) is a semester-long programme that is designed to aid students who have not yet decided on which course they would like to pursue. At the end of Year 1 Semester 1, DCITP students will have to make an informed choice in selecting one of the three IT courses available at School of Computing. In the whole span of the programme, students will take modules such as Fundamentals of Programming, Mathematics, Fundamentals of Computing and etc. The Diploma in Infocomm Security Management (DISM) is a 3-year full-time diploma course offered by the School of Computing in Singapore Polytechnic. This course allows students who are passionate in nullifying cyber threats to further develop their skills in doing so. The curriculum involved covers important aspects such as security technology, security management, law, teamwork and communication and information technology. Some of the modules that students will take are Fundamentals of Programming, Network Fundamentals, Linux Administration and Seurity, Applied Cryptography, Digital Forensics and Investigation, Ethical Hacking and Defences etc. Students in Year 2 or Year 3 of the course will be able to opt for the Industry Now Curriculum (INC) Pathway. After graduation, students of DISM will be able to hold job roles such as Security Engineer, Software Engineer and many more. The Diploma in Information Technology (DIT) is a 3-year full-time diploma course offered by the School of Computing in Singapore Polytechnic. This course provides students an opportunity to focus on Software and Applications development with three specialist tracks namely Software Development, User Experience (UX) and Immersive Simulation. Students will only have to pick one specialist track. This course allows students to also work with leading industry companies in Singapore such as Microsoft, Accenture, GovTech, DBS and many more through internship opportunities and project collaborations. Among the modules that students will have to take are Fundamentals of Programming, Front-End Web Development, Design for User Interaction, Back-End Web Development, Mobile Application Development etc. Students will also have to undertake the modules that have to be completed under the specialist track that they chose. Students in Year 2 or Year 3 of the course will be able to opt for the Industry Now Curriculum (INC) Pathway. The job roles that can be held by DIT graduates are Software Engineer, Scrum Master and many more.
